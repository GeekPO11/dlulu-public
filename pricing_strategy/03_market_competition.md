## 2. Competitor Rings

### Ring 1: Direct Competitors (AI Goal Planners)
*   **Motion**: Auto-scheduling. High price ($19-$34). Business focus.
*   **Reclaim.ai**: Calendar defense. Good freemium.
*   **Goblin.tools**: Breaking down tasks (Free/Donation). Very simple, no retention.

### Ring 2: Adjacent (Habits & Notes)
*   **Todoist**: The standard for lists. "Karma" gamification. Low price ($4-$6).
*   **Notion**: The "OS" for everything. AI is an add-on ($8-10).
*   **Cron / Notion Calendar**: Free calendar layer.

### Ring 3: Substitutes (The Status Quo)
*   **Google Gemini / ChatGPT**: "Write me a plan." (One-off, no state/tracking).
*   **Spreadsheets**: Infinite flexibility, zero intelligence.
*   **Mental Ops**: "I'll just remember it." (High failure rate).

2.  **Todoist ($5)** anchors the "List" value. We must be >$5 because we do "Intelligence", not just "Storage".
3.  **Notion AI ($10)** anchors the "AI Text" value. This validates our $10 price point perfectly.

## 5. What We Should Avoid
1.  **Surprise Bills**: No "Pay-as-you-go" overages. B2C users hate variability.
2.  **The "Tax on Curiosity"**: Don't charge for *generating* plans (the "Aha" moment). Charge for *managing* them over time.
3.  **Complex Tiers**: No "Gold/Platinum/Diamond". Just "Dreamer" (Free) and "Achiever" (Pro).
